Experience

2022 - 2024 | Scientist at Enable Medicine

Enable Medicine is a startup company delivering end-to-end spatial biology solutions, and building a cloud-based platform to index, annotate, and search multi-modal biological data.

Scaling and supporting internal data science flows

  • Authored and maintained internal and external-facing scientific packages for access and analysis of spatial omics data, including:
    • SpatialMap (R, closed source) [docs]
    • emobject (Python, open source) [docs, repo, my MRs]
  • Developed notebook templates to streamline analysis and reporting workflows for single-cell spatial proteomics
  • Translated one-off scripts and machine/deep learning frameworks into no-code user interfaces to reduce time to insight
  • Led workshop on git/GitLab, code review, and CI/CD practices for fellow scientists

Product development

  • Led technical design and execution of proof-of-concept pipeline to support new biological insights product
  • Collaborated with software engineering, product, and commercial teams to improve cloud platform usability and scope new features, including:
    • More intuitive data ingestion flows
    • Expanding data ingestion to new modalities
    • Improved workflows for annotation and visualization

R&D and solutions

  • Reviewed design docs and provided feedback on AI/ML research projects (Wu et al. 2023)
  • Executed and delivered custom single-cell spatial proteomics analysis for biopharma and academic clients, including QC, cell type classification/annotation, cell-cell interactions, and spatial analysis

2020 - 2022 | Postdoctoral Research Scholar at Stanford University

I conducted postdoctoral research in the lab of Sylvia Plevritis. I was awarded a Stanford Cancer Imaging Training (SCIT) fellowship to pursue hybrid wet/dry lab research characterizing how spatial patterns of cells and extracellular matrix change as a function of lung cancer progression.

  • Integrated multi-modal imaging data (multiplexed immunofluorescence, histology) to analyze lung cancer tissue
  • Established image analysis pipeline to align, process, and extract features describing tissue architecture
  • Applied machine learning techniques to identify tissue-based features associated with lung cancer progression and survival (manuscript in preparation)
  • Contributed to benchmarking methods for inferring cell-cell interaction from transcriptomics data (Yu et al. 2022)
  • Audited coursework in computational image analysis, statistical learning, AI in healthcare

2014 - 2020 | Graduate Research Assistant at Cornell University

I earned my Ph.D. in Biomedical Engineering in the lab of in the lab of Claudia Fischbach. I was awarded the Ruth L. Kirschstein Predoctoral Individual National Research Service Award (NIH F31) for my dissertation research, which focused on studying biological mechanisms of breast cancer metastasis to bone using spatially resolved approaches.

  • Led international collaboration to characterize cancer-associated changes in bone tissue using transcriptomics (bulk RNAseq) and multi-scale, correlative imaging (Chiou, Liu, et al. 2021)
  • Developed experimental imaging approach to visualize early metastatic cancer cells in bone tissue (Chiou, Hinckley, et al. 2021)
  • Contributed to 7 additional original research publications as a co-author
  • Mentored 4 undergraduate researchers
  • Completed coursework in cancer biology, tissue engineering, biomechanics, statistics, and science communication
